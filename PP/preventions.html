BREAKING DOWN STEREOTYPES





Some of the most damaging aspects to a woman's journey into STEM is the criticism and stereotypes she gets put on to them. These instances could be thought of to be as small as internalized sicrimination because it leads to an outer expression. This not only discourages the increase of female participation in STEM but also creates a dangerous working and living environment for the individual. A possible method of combatting this issue is by stopping and informing those around you whenever any negative gender based comment is stated. In addition, you can continue to educate yourself by listening to people who have experienced this first hand and evaluate the way you approach certain circumstanses while keeping their feelings in mind.

ENGAGING AT AN EARLY AGE

In many circumstances, it is best to concrete information into people at a very young age, and this is one of them. Throughout my earlier ages I remember STEM being heavily connected to the male population while women were more influenced into child care occupations and hobbies. As this expands and continues, the female STEM population gets cut off at the root and is given limited oppertunities to grow. I personally believe that if measures were taken to furhter persuade me into STEM at a young age I would have found one of my passions and interests. In other cases the lack of inclusion during the early stages completely stunts a female childs interests in expanding their knowledge of this topic. This can be easily combatted by including and encouraging everyone in STEM activities, clubs, and discussions.
